Mechanical exfoliation

The 2010 Nobel Prize was awarded to Andrei Geim and Konstantin Novoselov for initiating the field of "two-dimensional materials". These materials are a few atoms thick and exhibit amazing properties such as incredible mechanical strength, near-complete transparency to light and, under some circumstances, lossless conduction of electrical currents.

In the sixteen years since the original discovery there has been a tremendous amount of activity to learn more about this fascinating family of materials and develop industrial applications. One of the bottlenecks to this process has been obtaining samples of two-dimensional materials in the first place. The process identified by Geim and Novoselov, which remains the state-of-the-art, results in small pieces of atomically thin material scattered over wafers. Automating the process of finding promising candidates for experiments from tens of optical images would save hours weekly for the scientists.

Indentifying the candidate flakes is a computer vision task. In this app I employ a lightweight algorithm based on Linear Discriminant Analysis to good effect but more sophisticated approaches based on Convolutional Neural Networks have been found to work under some circumstances (see this paper, for example).
